Bluetooth Codecs Explained: SBC, aptX, aptX HD & AAC

Bluetooth transmits audio by compressing it using different codecs. SBC is the baseline codec and offers lower transfer rates and more audio quality loss. Qualcomm’s aptX and aptX HD increase the bit rate to 352 kbps and 576 kbps respectively, delivering clearer, more detailed audio than SBC. Apple’s preferred codec, AAC, uses psychoacoustic compression to achieve better quality, though performance on Android devices can vary.

Higher bitrates typically provide better sound, but they also require more bandwidth. Knowing which codecs your earbuds and devices support can help you choose the best combination for high‑quality wireless listening.
