Understanding Bluetooth Audio Codecs

A comprehensive guide to wireless audio quality and codec compatibility

What are Bluetooth Audio Codecs?

Bluetooth audio codecs are compression algorithms that determine how audio data is transmitted wirelessly from your source device (phone, computer) to your Bluetooth headphones or earbuds. The codec used affects audio quality, latency, and battery life.

Different devices support different codecs, and the final codec used depends on what both your source device and your EarFun audio device support. The devices will automatically negotiate and use the best mutually supported codec.

Common Bluetooth Audio Codecs

SBC (Subband Coding)

Compatibility: Universal - supported by all Bluetooth devices
Quality: Basic/Standard
Latency: ~200ms
Bitrate: Up to 328 kbps

SBC is the baseline codec that all Bluetooth devices must support. While it provides decent quality, newer codecs offer significant improvements in sound quality and efficiency.

AAC (Advanced Audio Coding)

Compatibility: iOS devices, some Android devices
Quality: Good
Latency: ~150ms
Bitrate: Up to 256 kbps

AAC is widely used and provides better compression efficiency than SBC. It's the preferred codec for Apple devices and delivers noticeably better audio quality.

aptX

Compatibility: Many Android devices, Windows PCs
Quality: Very Good
Latency: ~150ms
Bitrate: Up to 352 kbps

Developed by Qualcomm, aptX provides near CD-quality audio with lower latency than SBC. Many premium Android devices and EarFun products support this codec.

aptX HD

Compatibility: Select Android devices and PCs
Quality: Excellent
Latency: ~130ms
Bitrate: Up to 576 kbps

An enhanced version of aptX that supports high-resolution audio up to 24-bit/48kHz, providing audiophile-grade wireless sound quality.

aptX Low Latency

Compatibility: Gaming devices, some phones
Quality: Good
Latency: ~40ms
Bitrate: Up to 352 kbps

Designed specifically for applications requiring low latency, such as gaming or video watching. Reduces audio delay to near-imperceptible levels.

LDAC

Compatibility: Sony devices, some Android devices
Quality: Excellent
Latency: ~200ms
Bitrate: Up to 990 kbps

Developed by Sony, LDAC can transmit up to 3 times more data than standard codecs, supporting high-resolution audio up to 24-bit/96kHz.

How to Check Your Device's Codec

Android Devices

  1. Enable Developer Options in Settings
  2. Go to Settings > System > Developer Options
  3. Look for "Bluetooth Audio Codec" setting
  4. Connect your EarFun device and check the active codec

iOS Devices

iOS devices automatically select the best available codec. iPhone and iPad typically use AAC when available, falling back to SBC if needed.

Windows/Mac

Check your Bluetooth settings or use third-party apps to monitor the active codec. Many modern computers support aptX for high-quality audio.

Optimizing Your Audio Experience

Tips for Better Bluetooth Audio

  • Keep devices close: Maintain proximity between source and EarFun device for stable connection
  • Avoid interference: Minimize WiFi and other wireless interference
  • Update firmware: Keep your EarFun device firmware updated for latest codec support
  • Use quality sources: High-quality audio files benefit more from advanced codecs
  • Check compatibility: Ensure your source device supports the codecs your EarFun device can use

When Codecs Matter Most

  • Listening to high-resolution or lossless audio
  • Professional audio work or critical listening
  • Gaming where low latency is important
  • Video content where audio-video sync matters

Future of Bluetooth Audio

The Bluetooth Special Interest Group continues to develop new standards and codecs. Bluetooth LE Audio and the LC3 codec promise even better efficiency and quality in future devices.
